I design and teach undergraduate courses in comparative politics, with a focus on helping students build structured ways of analysing political systems, institutional change, and strategic behaviour. A core emphasis is developing students’ ability to think critically and evaluate competing explanations.

## Democratisation from a Comparative Perspective

- SoSe 2025 and 2026
- Explores how and why political systems transition to and from democracy across different historical and regional contexts. The course emphasises comparing theoretical frameworks and applying them to real-world cases.

## Democratic Development and Stability in Germany

- WiSe 23/24, 24/25, and 25/26
- Analyses the development and stability of German democracy in comparative perspective, linking political theory to institutional evolution and contemporary challenges.


## Small States in the International System: A Comparative Perspective

- SoSe 2022, 2023, and 2024
- Examines how small states operate within the international system, focusing on the constraints they face and the strategies they use to exert influence despite limited resources.
